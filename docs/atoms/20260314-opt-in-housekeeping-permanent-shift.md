# Opt-in housekeeping is a permanent industry shift, not a pandemic artifact

Contactless and opt-in housekeeping models, accelerated by the pandemic, have become entrenched as both a guest preference and a cost optimization strategy. This is not reverting to pre-2020 norms.

**The numbers**:
- 75% of guests prefer contactless check-in over in-person
- 77% prefer automated messaging for quick communication
- 54% of hotel executives want mobile check-in/out as a permanent standard
- 36% of hotels have already reduced daily room cleaning (driven by both labor shortages and guest preferences)
- Opt-in daily cleaning reduces labor costs by **15-30%**

**Opt-in models in practice**:
- **Opt-in daily cleaning**: Guests request housekeeping rather than receiving it by default. Now standard at many limited-service and mid-range brands.
- **Green programs**: Guests decline daily linen/towel changes for loyalty points, environmental cause donations, or small credits. Each towel reuse saves approximately 50 liters of water.
- **On-demand housekeeping**: Guests request cleaning via app, tablet, or text at their preferred time.
- **Scheduled cleaning windows**: Guests select a preferred time slot to avoid disruption.

**Revenue upside**: Properties offering app-based and kiosk services see approximately 70% higher upsell revenue per check-in compared to traditional front desk transactions. Mid-stay cleanings in vacation rentals are offered as upsells (additional revenue from what was previously a cost center).

**Implications for task management systems**: The shift from "clean every room every day" to "clean on request" fundamentally changes scheduling. Task generation can no longer be purely occupancy-driven — it must incorporate guest preferences, request timing, and opt-in/opt-out status. The Tasks domain needs guest request routing: guest communication creates housekeeping tasks dynamically rather than batch-generating them from the occupancy report.

This shift also connects to the labor shortage: opt-in housekeeping is partly a response to not having enough staff to clean every room daily. The two trends (labor shortage + guest preference for contactless) conveniently aligned, making the shift self-reinforcing.
