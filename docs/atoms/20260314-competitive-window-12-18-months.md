The strategic threat assessment gives AutoProxy a 12-18 month window before the competitive landscape closes meaningfully. Two forces define the timeline.

**The horizontal AI threat (12-18 months):** Microsoft Copilot and Slack AI are developing industry-specific capabilities, but the hospitality vertical buys time. Hotel internal operations messaging is too niche for Microsoft or Salesforce to prioritize -- their incentive is to serve the largest enterprise verticals first (finance, healthcare, legal). Industry-specific routing, schedule-aware assignment, and domain-trained classification are features that horizontal platforms will eventually build but will not build soon.

**The vertical competitor threat (faster, more dangerous):** Actabl (ALICE) is the biggest threat. They have hospitality domain knowledge and an existing install base. If they add an AI layer to their operations platform, they have the distribution and domain credibility to compete directly. However, Amos is built from scratch with AI in mind. ALICE would be bolting AI onto legacy operations tooling, which creates integration complexity and architectural constraints.

The window has implications for the build sequence. The recommended MVP (Phases 0-4, 10-15 weeks for on-site) plus first corporate phase (Phase 5, 3-4 weeks) fits within the window. But the full platform (16-23 weeks) consumes a significant portion of it. Speed on the routing layer is the highest priority -- that is where the position is established.

The window also means that AutoProxy's first-mover advantage is in establishing trust and adoption, not in building features. Once hotels are using AutoProxy for routing and classification, switching costs create retention even if competitors catch up on features.
