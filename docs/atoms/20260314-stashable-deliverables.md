The third validation pipeline principle states: "Every step produces a standalone markdown document committed to the knowledge base. No work is lost, even for killed products."

This is a meta-application of the Soloscaler philosophy. The knowledge base (Stasher) is the brain of the operation. Validation research is knowledge. Therefore, validation research belongs in the knowledge base, regardless of whether the product it evaluated survives.

Fifteen documents are produced per product across four waves: problem validation, market sizing, competitive landscape, audience validation, brand fit analysis, positioning stress test, technical feasibility, GTM path, unit economics, validation synthesis, and go/no-go recommendation (plus supporting documents).

The practical value: a killed product's market research, competitive analysis, and audience data remain available for future products. A competitive landscape analysis done for Product A informs Product B's positioning. An audience validation failure for one product reveals demand signals for a different product.

This principle also creates accountability. When every step produces a document, every step must produce substantive analysis — not just a verbal "this feels right" at a meeting. The document is the deliverable, and the deliverable is permanent.

The knowledge-base-first approach mirrors how Luhmann's Zettelkasten works: even discarded ideas contribute to the network because their connections illuminate relationships that surviving ideas benefit from. A killed product's validation notes become fodder for future strategic thinking.
