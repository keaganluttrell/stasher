# The 4-5 hour vacation rental turnover window defines VR operations

The single most defining constraint in vacation rental operations is the turnover window. With checkout typically at 10-11 AM and check-in at 3-4 PM, cleaners have a 4-5 hour window to completely reset a property for the next guest. Back-to-back bookings are common, making same-day turnovers the norm, not the exception.

This constraint shapes everything about VR operations:

**Task structure is turnover-dominated**: Unlike hotels with their mix of stayovers, checkouts, and deep cleans, VR operations revolve around one task type — the complete property reset. This includes full cleaning, linen change, amenity restock, and staging for photos/presentation. There are no stayover cleans (except for extended stays).

**Scheduling is calendar-driven**: Every turnover is driven by the reservation calendar. A missed turnover means a guest arrives to a dirty property — there is no front desk buffer or "your room will be ready in 30 minutes" option. The scheduling precision required is extreme.

**Staffing scales by property, not by room**: A 4-bedroom vacation home might need 2-3 cleaners for 3-5 hours, while a studio needs 1 cleaner for 1-2 hours. Teams are assigned per-property, not per-room.

**Cleaner reliability is critical**: Independent contractors may cancel or no-show. When the window is 4-5 hours and there is no backup, a cancellation is a crisis. Marketplace platforms like Turno (25,000+ cleaners) exist specifically to address this reliability gap.

**Geographic dispersion amplifies the constraint**: Properties spread across a region mean travel time between turnovers is significant. A cleaner cannot just walk to the next room down the hall — they may drive 20+ minutes between properties.

The turnover window constraint is relevant to Amos's Tasks domain design: task scheduling must account for hard deadlines (check-in time), not just workload balancing. A task that runs late in a hotel is an inconvenience; a task that runs late in a VR is a guest complaint on day one.
