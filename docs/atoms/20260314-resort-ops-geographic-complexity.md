# Resort operations are complex because of geographic spread and facility diversity

Resorts blend hotel-style room operations with expansive facility maintenance across a physically dispersed campus. This creates operational complexity that neither hotels nor vacation rentals face in isolation.

**Facility diversity**: Beyond rooms, resorts maintain pools, beaches, spas, multiple restaurants and bars, fitness centers, kids' clubs, sports courts, gardens, pathways, and outdoor furniture. Each facility type requires different cleaning standards, specialized equipment, and often different staff skill sets. Pool water quality testing is a regulatory requirement. Spa sanitation follows healthcare-adjacent standards. F&B hygiene inspections have their own cadence.

**Geographic spread**: Villas, bungalows, and facilities are dispersed across the property. Unlike a hotel where housekeeping moves floor-to-floor via elevator, resort teams may need golf carts or utility vehicles. Transit time between units is significant and must be factored into scheduling — a detail that most hotel-focused task management systems ignore entirely.

**Extended operating hours**: Resort amenities often run 16-20 hours daily. This creates complex shift coverage requirements. A hotel lobby needs cleaning during predictable periods; a resort pool area may need continuous attention from 6 AM to 10 PM.

**Longer average stays**: Resorts tend toward longer guest stays, which reduces turnover frequency but increases the complexity of stayover services. All-inclusive resorts have heavier public-area cleaning loads due to higher foot traffic across amenities.

**Seasonal demand spikes**: Many resorts face dramatic occupancy swings (peak season vs. off-season) requiring rapid staff scaling that hotels in urban areas do not face as acutely.

For Amos's Tasks domain, the resort use case means task locations cannot be limited to "rooms" — the system needs a flexible location model that handles rooms, facilities, zones, and outdoor areas. The existing Villas domain may need to be abstracted or extended to cover non-room locations.
