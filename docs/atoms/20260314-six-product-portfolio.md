Beyond Stacker and AutoProxy, the product-brand fit analysis recommends four new products that complete the soloscaler operating stack:

**Sentinel** — Automated brand and operations monitoring. Watches digital presence, vendor SLAs, review platforms, and key metrics. Only tells you when something actually needs attention. The "guardian while you sleep" product. MVP: 8-12 weeks. This fills the monitoring gap — a soloscaler cannot watch everything, so software must.

**Playbook** — Living operations manual builder. Turns workflows, SOPs, and tribal knowledge into a searchable, executable system. Self-Sufficiency as the Goal made into a product. Natural deliverable format for consulting work — every engagement produces a Playbook. MVP: 10-14 weeks.

**Cashview** — Real-time financial clarity layer. Connects bank, payment processor, and invoicing to deliver one daily answer: "Here is where your money is and where it is going." Not a full accounting system — just the view that matters. MVP: 10-14 weeks (compliance extends timeline).

**Dealflow** — Minimalist CRM for people who hate CRMs. Assumes sales is one of twelve things you do today. Interface is a daily briefing, not a database. "Here are the three deals that need you today." MVP: 6-10 weeks.

The sequencing is deliberate: AutoProxy first (smallest wedge), then Stacker + Playbook (operational backbone), then Sentinel + Cashview (monitoring layers), then Dealflow + horizontal expansion (full flywheel). Each phase builds on the last and creates demand for the next.

Total portfolio timeline: 18 months from first launch to full suite. Ambitious for a solo builder, but consistent with the thesis.
