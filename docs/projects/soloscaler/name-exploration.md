## Verdict: Keep "Soloscaler"

The name wins on every dimension that matters. It's an invented category word that Keagan can own entirely, and category creators always win in brand strategy.

## Why "Soloscaler" Works

- **Category creation**: The word doesn't exist in the market. Owning a new term means owning the category.
- **Self-explanatory**: "Solo" + "scaler" -- anyone can guess the meaning on first hearing.
- **Dual identity**: Describes what Keagan is AND what he helps clients become. Both credential and promise.
- **Linguistically flexible**: "I'm a soloscaler." "The soloscaler approach." "Soloscaler tools." Works as identity, methodology, and product line.
- **Narrative hook**: The name begs the question "what's a soloscaler?" -- the perfect brand story opening.

Other explored categories: leverage-themed (Levership, 10x Solo), builder-themed (Buildright, One-Builder), multiplier-themed (Forcemult, Amplifyd), and personal brand (Keagan.dev, KG Systems). None scored above 23.

## Managing the "Solo Implies Limitation" Risk

This is the one legitimate concern, addressed through messaging rather than renaming:

1. **Reframe as feature**: "Solo means you get me, not my junior associate. Zero bureaucracy. Senior-level thinking on every deliverable."
2. **Lead with results**: Case studies demonstrating solo-scale output neutralize the objection immediately.
3. **Embrace provocatively**: "Yes, I'm one person. That's the point. Let me show you why that's your advantage."
4. **Build the identity for clients**: When clients see themselves as soloscalers too, the concern disappears entirely.
