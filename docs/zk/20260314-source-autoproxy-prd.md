## Key Takeaways

- The "shoulder-tapping tax" is the core problem: hospitality operations run on interruptions that compound at every organizational level, from housekeepers walking to the front desk to CEOs buried in noise.
- AutoProxy makes four decisions per message: answer it, route it, absorb it, or escalate it. The vision is a "personal chief of staff" that handles triage.
- 10 personas span on-site (housekeeper, front desk, maintenance, GM, sales) and corporate (regional manager, COO, marketing, finance, HR), each with distinct pain points and message volumes.
- 22 user stories across 4 phases: Foundation (months 1-3), Learning (months 4-6), Intelligence (months 7-10), Autonomy (months 11-14).
- The Amos PMS advantage is total: real-time access to reservations, room status, tasks, schedules, rates, and org hierarchy. No external AI tool can match this.
- Success metrics are aggressive: 75% reduction in front desk interruptions by Phase 4, auto-response accuracy from 70% to 95%, executive triage time from 90min to 20min.
