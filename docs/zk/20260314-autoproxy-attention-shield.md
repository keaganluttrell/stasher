AutoProxy is called "the superpower" of SolOS and "the shield" in the five-tool metaphor. Its function: determine if an incoming message (email, text, chat, call) is worth the user's time or can be handled without human intervention.

The product thesis is captured in one scenario: "The client who texts you a question, figures it out two minutes later, and texts again to say never mind — that interaction never reaches you. AutoProxy handled it before you even knew it happened."

This is a product that sells itself through absence. The user's experience is not "look at this thing AutoProxy did" — it is "I had an uninterrupted afternoon and didn't realize why until I checked the logs." The value is invisible, which is both the product's strength and its marketing challenge.

AutoProxy has deep access to the full stack: Stasher (knowledge base for answers), Stacker (operations context for routing), Static (public content for consistency), and team context. This multi-source awareness is what separates it from a simple auto-responder. It does not just reply — it routes, prioritizes, and answers with context.

The attention-protection framing is counter-positioned against the rest of the AI tool market. Most AI tools promise to help you do more. AutoProxy promises to protect you from doing things you should never have been asked to do. This is a provocative inversion that aligns with the brand's overall contrarian positioning.

AutoProxy also powers the chat widget on Static sites, meaning a business's public face stays active even when the owner is heads-down on work. The owner's presence becomes asynchronous.
