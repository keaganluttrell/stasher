# Source: Housekeeping and Maintenance Operations Industry Research

Comprehensive industry research across hotels, vacation rentals, and resorts to inform the Amos Tasks domain. Covers operational workflows, technology trends, staffing models, quality assurance, preventive vs reactive maintenance, guest-facing innovations, pain points, required software features, integration architecture, KPIs, and competitive landscape.

## Key Takeaways

- The hospitality industry faces a severe labor shortage (65% of hotels report staffing gaps; housekeeping is hardest to fill at 38% shortage rate) driving technology adoption
- Mobile-first task management is non-negotiable across all segments — if it does not work on a phone, it is not viable
- The 4-5 hour vacation rental turnover window creates extreme scheduling pressure that hotels do not face
- Preventive maintenance delivers 25-35% cost reduction and 200-400% ROI within 18-24 months, yet reactive maintenance still dominates in practice
- PMS-native operations (zero integration friction) is the single biggest competitive differentiator — standalone ops tools lose to embedded ones
- Opt-in housekeeping has become permanent, not a pandemic artifact, reducing labor costs 15-30%
- The market is fragmented: no platform dominates across hotels, vacation rentals, and resorts — cross-segment flexibility is an underserved opportunity
- Reservation-driven automatic task generation is the #1 feature separating good ops software from bad
- Digital checklists with photo verification are industry standard for quality assurance
