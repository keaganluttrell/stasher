There is a structural communication fault line between corporate/HQ and property-level operations in hospitality, and it is getting worse as corporate functions go hybrid/remote while on-site operations remain fundamentally in-person.

The mismatch is temporal. Corporate staff work asynchronously -- in documents and email, across time zones. On-site staff work synchronously -- in real-time, in physical spaces. Messages from corporate arrive when the GM is in a guest interaction. Responses from properties arrive when the VP is in a board meeting. Neither party's communication rhythm matches the other's.

The evidence: Aimbridge Hospitality (1,500+ properties) uses separate systems for mobile communications (UKG Talk), enterprise BI (IBM), and guest comms (Medallia Zingle) -- no unified corporate-to-property messaging layer. Large chains like Marriott and Hilton have standardized PMS but corporate communication remains fragmented across email, Teams, and brand intranets. Mid-market companies cobble together email, WhatsApp groups, shared drives, and PMS reports.

Nearly half of hoteliers report difficulty accessing critical information. The "telephone game" problem makes it worse: when corporate launches a new rate or policy, properties interpret directives differently, configuration drift happens, and champions at each property burn out trying to be the bridge.

AutoProxy's opportunity is to be a temporal translator -- buffering corporate requests and surfacing them at the right time for on-site staff, aggregating on-site responses and summarizing them for corporate. This is not just routing optimization; it is bridging two fundamentally different work modes that existing tools treat as one.
