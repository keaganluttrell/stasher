Despite the steep growth in AI adoption (46.3% CAGR, $7.84B to $52.62B by 2030), ActivTrak data from March 2026 reveals a paradox: AI is making work harder, not easier. Email time is up 104%. Messaging time is up 145%. Focused work sessions are down 9%. BCG has identified "AI brain fry" -- cognitive overload from managing multiple AI tools that each generate more outputs, more summaries, and more messages.

This is a critical market insight for AutoProxy's positioning. Most AI tools are addition layers: they summarize after the fact, generate more content, create more notifications. Microsoft Copilot summarizes your Teams chats. Slack AI recaps your channels. Both help you process messages faster after receiving them. Neither reduces the number of messages you receive.

AutoProxy is positioned as a reduction layer -- "less messaging, powered by AI." It intercepts, filters, routes, and auto-responds before messages reach you. The value proposition is not "AI for messaging" but "less messaging, powered by AI." This counter-positioning against the prevailing trend is the entire thesis.

The paradox also suggests timing is favorable. Organizations that adopted first-generation AI tools and saw workloads increase are now primed for second-generation tools that promise the opposite. The pain of the paradox creates the buying signal for the solution.
