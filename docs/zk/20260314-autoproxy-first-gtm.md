The product-brand fit analysis makes a decisive recommendation: AutoProxy should launch first, not Stacker. The reasoning follows three criteria:

**1. Smallest surface area.** AutoProxy solves one problem — protecting the user's attention from interruptions. It is the smallest product to build, test, and iterate. Stacker, by contrast, spans 12+ business domains and carries significant scope risk for a solo builder.

**2. Universal relatability.** Every knowledge worker understands "275 interruptions a day." The problem AutoProxy solves transcends industries and business sizes. Stacker requires explaining a composable business stack. AutoProxy requires explaining nothing — people already feel the pain.

**3. Most brand-aligned.** AutoProxy IS the tagline made into software. "One person. Unfair advantage." — and the unfair advantage is that your AI proxy handles the noise while you do the work. The positioning line is: "AutoProxy is not another AI tool that helps you do more. It's the first one that protects you from doing things you should never have been asked to do."

The go-to-market sequence builds from this wedge:
- Phase 1 (months 1-4): AutoProxy standalone + Stacker integration
- Phase 2 (months 4-8): Stacker core + Playbook
- Phase 3 (months 8-14): Sentinel + Cashview
- Phase 4 (months 14-18): Dealflow + horizontal expansion

AutoProxy should exist as both a standalone product (where the brand story lives) and a native Stacker integration (where the flywheel lives). The standalone version acquires users. The Stacker integration retains them and cross-sells the broader platform.
