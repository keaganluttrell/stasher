The SolOS product suite positions time reclamation as the single problem the entire stack solves: "Whether you are a soloscaler fielding client questions or a startup bleeding hours to shoulder-tapping and disorganization, SolOS eliminates the noise and keeps the signal strong."

This is a smart unifying thesis because time is the one resource a soloscaler cannot scale. Money can be leveraged (invest $1, get $5 back). People can be leveraged (one system serves many). But time is zero-sum for an individual — every hour spent on interruptions is an hour not spent on high-leverage work.

Each tool maps to a specific time leak:
- **Stasher** eliminates time spent searching for and re-deriving information
- **Staffer** eliminates time spent doing work that agents can handle
- **Stacker** eliminates time lost to context-switching between disconnected tools
- **Static** eliminates time spent manually maintaining a public presence
- **AutoProxy** eliminates time spent on interruptions that do not need human attention

The "signal vs. noise" metaphor runs through the entire document. SolOS keeps the signal strong. This frames the user's attention as the scarce resource and the stack as a filter that protects it.

Time reclamation also connects to the broader Soloscaler thesis. If one person can do the work of ten, the bottleneck is not capability — it is time allocation. The person has the skills. They just need systems that prevent their time from being consumed by low-value interruptions.
