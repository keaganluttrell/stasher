The competitive landscape for AutoProxy has three tiers, and the gap between them is the opportunity.

**Tier 1: Horizontal Platform AI (massive, generic).** Microsoft Copilot in Teams and Slack AI (powered by Claude since January 2026) summarize chats, generate recaps, and surface insights. Gap: they summarize after the fact. They do not intercept, route, or auto-respond proactively. They are reading assistants, not shielding assistants.

**Tier 2: Shared Inbox / Triage Tools (closer, external-facing).** Front and Missive combine inbox management with AI features. Gap: designed for external customer communication, not internal team messaging. No domain awareness, no confidence-scored auto-response.

**Tier 3: Hospitality-Specific Platforms (domain-aware, no AI proxy layer).** ALICE by Actabl is the closest competitor -- an all-in-one operations platform for housekeeping, service delivery, guest messaging, and internal comms. Won #1 Concierge Software in 2026 HotelTechAwards. Gap: task routing and communication are manual or rule-based. No AI triage, no auto-response, no "shield" concept. Canary Technologies auto-responds to 80%+ of guest inquiries but is entirely guest-facing with zero internal operations capability.

The gap matrix is clear: the only tool that combines message summarization, AI-driven intelligent routing, internal auto-response, confidence scoring, hospitality domain awareness, and noise filtering is AutoProxy. Every competitor is missing at least three of these capabilities.

This gap will not last forever. The biggest threat is ALICE adding an AI layer to their existing install base. The 12-18 month window before horizontal AI develops industry-specific routing is the available time to establish the position.
