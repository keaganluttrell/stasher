The competitive landscape shifts dramatically when AutoProxy expands from on-site to corporate use cases. This is a fundamental strategic insight that should govern the build sequence.

**On-site competitors (current threat surface):** Beekeeper (frontline comms, Maia AI assistant -- medium threat, broadcast-oriented), Workvivo/Zoom (employee experience, AI Companion -- low threat, engagement not ops), Flip (frontline comms, AI-native -- medium threat, no PMS integration), and hotel PMS tools (basic notifications -- low threat, no communication intelligence). These tools lack AI. AutoProxy's advantage is intelligence.

**Corporate competitors (new threat surface):** Microsoft Copilot + Teams (320M users, full LLM integration -- HIGH threat), Slack AI (42M DAUs, strong mid-market -- HIGH threat), Superhuman (executive email speed + AI triage -- medium threat, email-only), Lindy (autonomous AI assistant -- medium threat, horizontal), and the $5.46B AI email assistant market growing at 20.9% CAGR. These tools have AI. AutoProxy's advantage is hospitality context.

The critical insight: the wedge is different at each level. On-site, you win because you have AI and they don't. Corporate, you win because you have hospitality data and they don't. If you abandon the PMS anchor and try to be a generic corporate communication tool, you lose to Microsoft in 6 months.

This means scope discipline is the critical strategic constraint for the corporate expansion. Every corporate feature must be evaluated against the question: does this leverage PMS operational data as the core differentiator? If the answer is no, you are building a feature that Microsoft, Slack, or Superhuman can replicate faster and distribute wider.
