The primary tagline decision landed on "One person. Unfair advantage." Selected for two qualities:

**Versatility.** It works in a website hero section, a Twitter bio, an email signature, a business card, and a conference badge. Most taglines are context-dependent. This one compresses into any format without losing meaning.

**Intrigue.** It makes someone ask "what do you mean?" — which is the optimal response to a tagline. A tagline that explains everything leaves nothing to explore. A tagline that provokes curiosity creates an opening for the brand story. "One person. Unfair advantage." raises the question: what makes one person an unfair advantage? That question leads directly into the Soloscaler thesis.

The secondary tagline "Scale without the headcount" serves a different purpose: clarity. For ad copy, landing page subheads, and contexts where intrigue is less valuable than immediate understanding, this tagline communicates the value proposition directly.

Other strong options considered: "Big company firepower. Small business soul." (emotional but long), "Built for the lean. Not the lazy." (provocative but potentially alienating), "Outscale everyone. Outhire no one." (clever but too cute).

The primary/secondary pairing is strategic. Lead with intrigue in brand-building contexts (social, about pages, keynotes). Lead with clarity in conversion contexts (ads, landing pages, proposals). Never use both simultaneously — they serve different moments in the buyer journey.
