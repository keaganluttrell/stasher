## Key Takeaways

- AI agent market growing at 46.3% CAGR, but AI adoption is actually increasing workloads (email +104%, messaging +145%), creating demand for a reduction layer rather than another addition layer.
- Nobody is building an AI proxy layer for internal operations messaging in hospitality. The competitive landscape has three tiers: horizontal AI (Copilot, Slack AI), shared inbox tools (Front, Missive), and hospitality platforms (ALICE, Canary) -- none combine AI intelligence with hospitality domain awareness for internal comms.
- Five hospitality-specific characteristics make generic tools fail: wildly varying time-criticality, role-based routing needs, extremely common repeating patterns, shift-based workforce, and multilingual teams.
- Research shows professionals who detect AI-generated communication lose trust in the sender, making transparency and conservative thresholds essential.
- Biggest threat is Actabl (ALICE) adding AI to their existing operations platform. Window estimate: 12-18 months before horizontal AI develops industry-specific routing.
