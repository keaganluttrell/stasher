## Key Takeaways

- The corporate communication overload problem is real and quantified: C-level executives receive 3x more messages than ICs, knowledge workers lose 5 working weeks per year to context switching, U.S. businesses lose $650B annually to communication-driven distraction.
- A structural communication fault line exists between corporate and property: corporate works async, on-site works sync, and no existing tool bridges this gap intelligently.
- Expanding corporate changes the competitive dynamics: on-site, you compete with tools lacking AI; corporate, you compete with AI tools lacking hospitality context (Microsoft, Slack, Superhuman).
- Report intelligence is the recommended first corporate feature because it is most tied to the PMS data moat and least replicable by horizontal tools.
- Corporate AutoProxy could command $25-50/user/month for executive tiers, with an addressable market of approximately $10-15M ARR from multi-property management companies alone.
- Scope discipline is critical: the moment AutoProxy tries to be a generic corporate communication platform without the PMS anchor, it loses to companies with $100B+ in resources.
